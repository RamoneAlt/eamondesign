What is East Africa's Water Crisis?

The water crisis refers to the shortage of safe and accessible water resources in the region. Countries such as Kenya, Ethiopia, Somalia, and Sudan all face challenges in meeting the water needs of their populations. Factors such as climate change, population growth, and political instability contribute to the crisis. Insufficient water availability leads to health issues, limited agricultural productivity, and constraints to overall development.

The Scope of the Problem

The drought sweeping over East Africa is the worst in over 40 years. With rainy seasons failing, time and time again, this leaves very little to no rain. Drought is causing a lack of good vegetation and growth within agricultural settings. Livestock unable to eat and reproduce has left many regions in a severe famine. This led to millions of people in East Africa being left without food.

Flooding

Droughts causing water scarcity aren't the only issue in East Africa. Flooding is also a primary concern in Ethiopia and Somalia. At the beginning of March 2023, these countries began to get some rain which was a sign of hope, or at least it was until heavy rain started pouring in April. The heavy rainfall came so suddenly that it overflowed into the Shebelle and Jube rivers killing dozens and affecting over 300,000 people in Somalia and Ethiopia. Flooding led to almost 100,000 hectares of crops destroyed and the death of over 20,000 livestock.

Health Risks

Access to clean drinking water has improved globally, with 91% of the population having access to better water sources by 2015. However, 663 million people still rely on unsafe water sources, leading to many waterborne diseases. By 2025, it is estimated that half of the world's population will live without adequate access to fresh water. In low-middle-income countries, a significant number of healthcare facilities lack proper water sources, sanitation, and hygiene facilities, posing additional challenges to public health.

Health Risks

Water is necessary for your body as it regulates body temperature, protects the body and tissues, carries nutrients and oxygen to your cells, and more. However, contaminated water can transmit many diseases such as:

  • Cholera
  • Typhoid
  • Polio
  • Diarrhea
  • Dysentery

    • These waterborne diseases alone are responsible for approximately 502,000 deaths annually.

Possible Solutions

By adopting innovative agricultural practices, improving water management, and utilizing groundwater resources, East Africa can work towards building resilience, ensuring food security, and securing a sustainable water future for its communities. Some possible ways to do this include:

  • Promoting the growth of drought-resistant crops that require less water.
  • Using organic matter in fertilizers to improve water retention in soil.
  • Making wells to make water more accessible for communities.
  • Drilling for groundwater.
