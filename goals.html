Why Personal Goals Are Important

Water scarcity and sanitation challenges are global issues. While you may not feel the immediate impact, it's necessary to recognize their worldwide consequences. By raising awareness and conserving water, we can address these crises on a global scale. Let's make a difference and prevent worsening water shortages worldwide.

What Can You Do As An Individual?

Your actions matter. By conserving water, adopting sustainable habits, and raising awareness, you can make the change. Here you can read about ways to save water and help the world achieve the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal #6, "ensure availability of and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all."

Water Saving Strategies

Switching to water-efficient appliances

One way to reduce water usage is by using more up-to-date and water-efficient appliances. Doing so will decrease the water you waste in your day-to-day life. Some of the water-efficient appliances you can use include:

  • Water-efficient shower heads

    • Can decrease the average usage of water every minute from 2.5 liters to around 1.5 liters or, in other words, you'd save up to 20,000 liters of water per year.

  • Water aerators in faucets

    • Low water aerators reduce the flow of a sink to save water that would usually get wasted. The average bathroom sink expells 9 liters of water per minute saving 450% of the water used when a low water aerator is installed.

  • Low-flow toilets

    • Using low-flow toilets can drastically decrease the water wasted when you flush the toilet. According to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), older toilets use up to 6 gallons of water per flush! However, more recently made low-flow toilets use around 1.5 gallons per flush, proving to be a great water-saver with up to 13,000 gallons of water a year being saved.

Conscious Water Consumption

Being conscious of your water consumption is a crucial step to saving water. Bad habits in daily activities like keeping on the tap while brushing your teeth or loading the dishwasher before it's filled waste water. And the amount of water this waste adds up every single day. Here are some ways to reduce your water usage in your daily life:

  • Turning off the tap when brushing

    • Believe it or not, keeping the tap on while brushing your teeth consumes lots of water. For every minute you keep the tap on while brushing up to 9 liters of water are used up. That's nearly 5,300 liters (or 1,400 gallons) of water a year wasted while brushing your teeth!

  • Taking shorter showers

    • On average, showers make up roughly 22% of a household's water use, so reducing your shower time is a great way to reduce water usage.

    Aiming for 4-minute showers is spectacular, but even going to under 10 minutes is practical. Some other ways to conserve water include switching the showerhead off when using soap or shampoo or collecting water that would've gone down the drain to water plants.

Harvesting Rainwater

Although there are many ways to conserve water, there are also ways to harvest your water by using rain barrels. Using rain barrels can, on average, save a homeowner around 1,300 gallons of water; this can reduce your dependency on public water and also allows for a quick solution for water to water plants or your garden.

Savvy Sprinkler Solutions

Outdoor water usage accounts for 30% of the average household's water usage nationwide. Or roughly over 8 billion gallons a day from residential outdoor water use. Sprinkler systems require a lot of water, so here are some ways to decrease your sprinkler's water usage:

  • Install water-efficient sprinklers.

    • WaterSense-labeled sprinklers can save the average household around 5,600 gallons of water per year. On a larger scale, if every home using sprinklers in the U.S. switched to WaterSense sprinklers, it would save over 31 billion gallons of water yearly.

  • Inspect Sprinklers

    • Checking your sprinkler systems on a regular basis is a good idea, as a broken sprinkler head can waste around 25,000 gallons of water in just six months, according to the EPA

Educate Others

Educating others about the importance of saving water is crucial in creating an impact. By sharing knowledge and awareness, you can empower individuals and communities to adopt water-saving habits that can significantly reduce water consumption. Encouraging responsible water usage and promoting efficient practices conserves a vital resource and contributes to poverty reduction, improved well-being, and a healthier planet. By inspiring others to take action, we create a ripple effect of positive change, leading to a more sustainable future for all.
